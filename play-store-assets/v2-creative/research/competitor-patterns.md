# Competitor and market patterns

Review date: 2026-07-22. This was a brief directional review; visual production remained the primary
workstream.

Current PDF-category leaders consistently front-load one broad all-in-one promise, then move into
specific workflows such as scanning, reading, editing, signing, and organizing. Adobe Acrobat's
listing emphasizes its breadth and reader/editor position, while Xodo explicitly presents a combined
reader, scanner, editor, and business workflow. iLovePDF similarly organizes its pitch around scanner,
converter, editor, and document-organization jobs.

Sources:

- [Adobe Acrobat Reader on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.adobe.reader)
- [Xodo PDF Reader & Editor on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.xodo.pdf.reader)
- [iLovePDF on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.ilovepdf.www)

QuietPDF should not compete through a denser tool grid. The V2 response is a calmer narrative:
understand the product, see two strong transformation outcomes, trust the reader and compression
workflows, then close on privacy and a clear saved result. Before/after comparisons, large document
previews, and authentic controls carry the story faster than feature-count claims.

Google's current Play guidance requires a 512×512 high-resolution icon (maximum 1024 KB) and a
1024×500 feature graphic. Google applies the Play mask and shadow dynamically, which supports the
decision to ship a full-bleed square icon without a baked rounded outer mask or shadow.

- [Google Play preview asset requirements](https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9866151)
- [Google Play icon design specifications](https://developer.android.com/distribute/google-play/resources/icon-design-specifications)
